Master of Commerce (M. Com. Program) Overview


The Master of Commerce (M.Com) is a postgraduate program that allows students to explain their multifaceted skills in various contexts. The program aims to acquire knowledge through action and give a practical and current view based on a solid theoretical background. To address the challenges of today's complex world, our mission is to assist students in developing a solid sense of self-concept and gaining the required inner and outward life skills to face the obstacles that life throws at them confidently.

Master of Commerce (M.Com) is a two-year full-time (4 semesters) postgraduate program offered by Seshadripuram College Bangalore, best colleges for M.Com in Bangalore affiliated to Bangalore University. Commerce, Management, Economics, Accounting, and Finance are topics covered in the curriculum. It improves commerce's theoretical, analytical, and applied knowledge base and students' advanced theoretical comprehension and research talents, preparing them for academic and research professions. Subject experts give industry-specific pedagogy that provides students with a wide range of managerial abilities while growing competency in a specific field.

This degree typically involves a thesis component, but it could also be entirely research-based. The course improves commerce's theoretical, analytical, and applied knowledge foundation. Accounting, finance, information systems, and computerized accounting are all included in the Master of Commerce. This program is for students who want to expand their expertise in their chosen field. M.Com differs from other business and management degrees in that it lays a greater emphasis on theory, with less emphasis on practice at times. This M.Com program will also prepare students for academic and research careers by providing them with excellent subject-matter competence in Commerce and Management and advanced theoretical understanding and research capabilities.

The Master's degree program includes the study of accounting principles, economic theory, micro and macroeconomics, money and banking systems, knowledge of business, commerce, and the regulations that govern it, and so on. Students choose to specialize in one of the commerce sectors in their final year, such as statistics, taxation, marketing, accounting and finance, banking, insurance, etc. Simply put, M.com is a postgraduate degree program that focuses on systematically studying ideas such as accounting, business studies, statistics, economics, mathematics, finance, banking, law, taxation, marketing, and management studies, among others. As a result, pursuing an M.Com degree provides insight into the fields of business, management, and economics.
